Ella Sun

Animal Farm

2D, 3D, MIXED MEDIA · 2026

Art is a process. It is a journey of the soul that seeks to express its truths, wishes, and emotions. As many things excite my mind, I know that my journey will be endless.


Art is freedom. Endlessness means unlimited possibilities. As a bird flies in a limitless sky, I am free to run as far as I wish, whenever I wish to. That freedom will lead me to the truth I want to find.


Art is love. It is the manifestation of one's passion for the beautiful and wondrous, for everything in existence. I want to embrace all the aspects of life and prove my love for them through my art.


The Decay of Probability (12 x 16 inches. Acrylic, gesso, sumi ink.) Inspired by Agnes Martin's Personages, 1952 lithograph piece, this was the first painting I created in the quantum series. It imagines what an instantaneous event—decay of probability—might look like.


Still You (30 x 24 inches. Oil, burlap cloth, mirror on canvas.) This is the first painting that connects entanglement to human connections. The mirror reflects the viewer, as this piece is a message to its audience: no matter how much time passes, no matter what changes, you are still yourself, just like how a quantum system functions.


Looking Across The Universe at You (30 x 40 inches. Oil and mirror on canvas.) The biggest piece of the series. Two quantum systems are personified and "entangled" through the subtraction of 2-D space, where they converge in a singular eye that reflects the viewer's gaze.


Binary (12 x 14 x 12 inches. Oil and thread on canvas and wood frame with a mirror base.) The first to explore entanglement through sculpture. The canvas represents an on state (1), and the frame represents an off state (0). Together and entangled, they make up our binary world.


Quantum Dots (4 x 6 inches. Oil on wood planks.) Inspired by Agnes Martin's work, I moved to much smaller canvases and experimented with layering paint. The dots depict the fluctuations that are key to quantum technology.


Resonance (12 x 12 inches. Oil on wood.) Two entangled entities can "feel" each other, no matter the distance that separates them and the chaos that surrounds them. I wanted to depict this phenomenon in a romantic light.


Embrace (12 x 12 x 12.5 (blue), 14 (red) inches. Wood, sculpey, acrylic, assorted beads.) Imagine that if an entangled system can feel each other from across the universe, they must yearn to connect with their partner. They would emerge from their shadows and head for each other's light.


Interstellar (9 x 10 x 12 inches. Metal, semisphere beads, acrylic on sculpey.) An experimental piece that shows the "closeness" of an entangled system. Despite being on different planes of space in the grand cosmos, they are still connected.
