Senna Reyes

Untitled

CERAMICS · 2026

As I wrap up this final term of art, I wanted to push myself to produce as many pieces as possible with the time I had left. Across all three terms, my work kept returning to the same inspiration: my summer travels. Everything from California to the Philippines to Thailand—each place shaped the way I created and experimented. Each tile is unique, showcasing different techniques and interpretations of the architecture and nature that I came across. I wanted my pieces to look like paintings from afar, but when you take a step closer, you discover that it's sculptural, textured, and dimensional.


The whole process, however, was far from effortless. I had my fair share of failures and disappointments. I spent the fall term trying out different methods of sculpting and glazing on miniature tiles, convinced that once I mastered the basics, I'd be able to crank out tile after tile without any trouble. I didn't realize that even in the winter and spring terms, things could still fall apart. Tiles exploded in the kiln, glaze reacted unpredictably, and pieces cracked beyond repair. There were moments where this class felt like one of the most exasperating ones I've ever taken. But I kept my head up and kept going and tried again. There's a certain patience that this class has taught me, and with these final completed pieces, I ended up with work that I'm very proud of.
