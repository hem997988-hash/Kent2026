Jack Williams

This and That

2D · 2026

During my time in AS Studio Art, I created four artworks: one in the spring of my junior year and three during my senior year.

My first piece was a color digital artwork depicting my house in Manchester, England. I created this piece in Photoshop, using a digital stylus as a versatile tool to replicate a range of traditional artistic techniques. This work focused on capturing structure and atmosphere through controlled digital rendering.

My second piece, titled "This and That," was inspired by a local curry house near my home in the UK. For this artwork, I intentionally shifted my style. I designed the composition around a central focal point rendered in color, surrounded by cross-hatched buildings and details that function as visual "noise." This contrast allowed the focal point to stand out more prominently. I began this piece over the summer between my junior and senior years as a personal project, initially not intending to continue in this style. However, I grew to appreciate the way textured "noise" could fill space while enhancing emphasis on more detailed elements.

In the winter, I transitioned from drawing to constructing a physical model. I created a model of my dorm room with the intention of passing it down each year, allowing future occupants to leave their signatures as a record of its history. Although I will not be displaying this piece, due to its later use as a temporary habitat for Yurtle, the North Dorm turtle, who has since been returned to the creek, the process was highly valuable. I gained practical insight into materials, discovering, for example, that balsa wood is effective for small-scale furniture construction and that popsicle sticks can be adapted into miniature drawers.

My final piece was an architectural drawing of the Rectory, Mrs. O'Dwyer's house behind Foley. In this work, I aimed to move beyond a single fixed perspective by combining elements of a floor plan, bird's-eye view, and multiple angles within one composition. I continued to develop my use of "noise," rendering the house in fine detail while keeping the surrounding landscape more loosely drawn to guide the viewer's focus toward the central structure.

Overall, my experience in AS Studio Art was highly rewarding. Mrs. Summers and Ms. Lynch were instrumental in my development as an artist. Despite a minor incident this fall involving desks, paint, and a flag, which I still apologize for, I am grateful for their guidance and support throughout the course.
