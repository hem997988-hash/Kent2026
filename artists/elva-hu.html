Elva Hu

Circular Serenity

2D, 3D · 2026

This year in AS Architecture, my work explores how architecture can make visible forms of memory that are often intangible: remembrance, loss, recurrence, and the temporal rhythms embedded in nature. Across these projects, I investigate space not as a static enclosure but as a medium through which time can be felt, ritualized, and reimagined.


Rather than understanding time as a linear progression, I approach it through return, repetition, and continuity. In "I Will Love You in Every Version of Time," the looping logic of a Möbius strip and a rising stair imagines death not as rupture, but as a point of meeting within an ongoing emotional continuum. This interest continues in "Tell Me Again about the People We Lost," where a ritual sequence of journey, judgment, purification, return, and remembrance explores how architecture can give form to mourning while allowing memory to persist.


Nature serves not simply as context in my work, but as a generative system. In What Plants Already Know?, I study organic cross-sections of vegetables and fruits as latent spatial orders, translating natural geometries into architectural form. In Circular Serenity, a meditation house extending from land to water uses circular movement to emphasize stillness, continuity, and coexistence with nature.


Memory is the thread binding these investigations together. I am interested in how movement, atmosphere, and material presence can preserve traces of lived experience and create spaces for reflection. Together, I position architecture as an interface between nature and memory, where spatial form can mediate presence across time.
