Jayda Wang

Perched Perspectives

PHOTOGRAPHY · 2026

Torn images float mischievously in resin. I wait to see what shimmering surprise will surface. Sometimes it is an image unfolding forgotten memories; other times it is random shapes revealing unfamiliar landscapes. The act of collaging is like holding uncertainty until new meanings have been uncovered—it is a journey of wandering through whispers of consciousness.


When making a collage, what excites me most is when two distinct elements come together to create something unexpected and meaningful. Collaging torn photographs became the start of my fascination with psychology. I was interested in understanding the mental mechanisms behind my instincts. As images surfaced from different stages of my life, layers of memory unfolded non-linearly, each revealing a different narrative. Through psychology, I came to understand that human perception is always fragmented. Based on this idea, I created my series Compass, using torn photographs as a metaphor for self-expression and exposing the kaleidoscopic nature of identity.


During Advanced Studies, my practice expanded from collage into photography, installation, and moving image. I began to focus not only on the image itself, but on how meaning is constructed through layering, material, and space. In works like Perched Perspectives, I experimented with resin, sculpture, and projection to create shifting viewpoints, allowing the viewer to experience the work rather than simply observe it. This process pushed me to move beyond making images into constructing environments where perception is constantly changing.


The shifting cultural histories of East Asia continue to shape my photography practice. Traveling through China, Hong Kong, and Japan, I feel that I embody the perspective of both an artist and an observer, framing a present that is constantly shaped by echoes of the past. In works like Hong Kong Diptych, contrasting lights, hanging fish bags, and small details reveal the quiet reality behind a familiar place. I am drawn to these moments where chance, timing, and environment come together to create something unplanned yet meaningful.


From my spontaneous exploration of collage, I have come to see how fragments—of images, memories, and histories—can align to form meaning. Advanced Studies has allowed me to expand this idea across different materials and disciplines. I continue to explore how art can transform observation and research into something that can be felt, experienced, and reinterpreted.
