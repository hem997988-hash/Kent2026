Untitled

PHOTOGRAPHY · 2026

I took these photographs without expectations. I had brought my camera along on a fishing trip, thinking I might take a few pictures if the moment felt right. But when I saw the vividly colored ducks moving through a cold, quiet landscape, to me, this moment was more than just a photograph.


These ducks, living and persevering through one of the harshest seasons, became a source of inspiration for me. What stood out most was that they were never alone; each one had at least one other by its side. In a frozen, lifeless environment, there was still connection, movement, and quiet resilience.


Nature has always been an important part of my life. Growing up, I spent hours outside in the woods, so long that my parents would sometimes worry. As I got older, I drifted away from that connection. Recently, I've been trying to return to it, mostly through fishing. Bringing my camera with me has become part of that. I've started to see nature not just as a place, but as something that communicates. There is a quiet beauty in being by the river, surrounded by the woods, that I wanted to capture and share.


Ultimately, I wanted to share both the resilience of wildlife in winter and my own effort to reconnect with something I had nearly lost.
