Nuria Surosh

Untitled

PAINTING, CERAMICS · 2026

The main inspiration for this theme was Ambrosius Bosschaert, a famous Dutch painter from the 1600s. He is well known for his incredibly realistic still life paintings of flowers and fruit. I was drawn to his style because his flowers look almost like photographs, which inspired me to focus on detail and color in my first watercolor project. For my second project, I decided to blend this painting style with my cultural background by incorporating Persian poetry. In Persian literature, flowers are powerful symbols of beauty and emotion. Growing up, I noticed that almost every poem I read used different flowers to express a feeling or tell a story. I wanted to show how art and poetry are naturally connected by creating this piece.


For my spring project, I was inspired by the delicate 18th-century Qajar art style, especially the royal portraits of women from that era. I wanted to challenge myself by trying something new, so I decided to combine painting with ceramics. I painted a portrait of an Afghan woman wearing detailed, traditional clothing on a ceramic platter. This was my first time ever blending these two mediums. My goal was to take the elegant style of old Persian paintings and apply it to a new medium to celebrate Afghan culture.
