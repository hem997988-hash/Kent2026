Lucas Husted-Edwards

Kent School's Chapel

PHOTOGRAPHY · 2026

My work explores "Painting with Light" through long exposure photography. This helped change basic photos into layered images. Rather than capturing a single moment in a second, I captured the image throughout a timely setting. This allowed motion, color, and energy to help enhance the image within a single frame. With a few of these images, light becomes my main subject, tracing movement, revealing patterns to the naked eye, and turning a blank image into an abstract niche perspective.


After capturing many perspectives of long exposures, I switched over to landscapes. Using the Kent School's Chapel as a main focus point, it showed the contrast between a nice sunny day and cold, snowy weather, as the trees added an appealing similarity to the tower. The natural weather shifting with the light and the absence of people make the image have a calm appearance. Looking at the landscapes of Kent from different angles adds additional beauty to the "Lovely Valley Land" many get to call home.


My interest in sports comes from the love of the sport; I am grateful to be able to play ice hockey at such a high level and be seen as a leader by my peers. These types of images introduce a different kind of energy to photography. Instead of slow shutter speeds as I used for long exposures, I used fast shutter speeds to capture the motions of the players. Athletes were captured in moments of repetition, speed, and physical intensity, showcasing the poetry in motion.


Ultimately, I hope to invite the viewers to see beyond the lens by stretching and compressing time, using light as a strong force, and creating images that feel abstract while encouraging a calm point of view.
