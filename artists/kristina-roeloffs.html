Kristina Roeloffs

Untitled

2D, MIXED MEDIA · 2026

Art has been a form of expression for centuries, and it continues to serve that purpose for me. I settled on mixed media as my personal form of expression a few years ago after experimenting with monoprint collages in England. That experimentation left a lasting impact on my art, and I have found myself favoring it as a technique and medium. Mixed media is the use of more than one medium in an art piece, for example, combining oil painting and paper collage. The subject of my art varies, but I began incorporating themes of nature after receiving it as a prompt for the direction of my work, and I've continued with it due to the impact it had on me as an artist.

When I first started utilizing nature as a key theme in my art, I was looking at architecture and how nature could meld with the man-made to create livable and biophilic spaces. As I continued to explore the combination of natural versus artificial, it became a significant part of what I considered my personal art. After moving on from architecture, I returned to mixed media, specifically collage, and the freedom offered by collage as a medium is what inspires me to create. I find that my artwork, when it comes to mixed media, tends to have a given meaning and interpretation behind it without being visually realistic. I assign meaning to my art as I work on it, and my hope is that my viewers can also recognize a deeper meaning other than aesthetics, even if their interpretation is not my own.
