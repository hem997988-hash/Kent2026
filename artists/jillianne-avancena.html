Jillianne Avancena

Designing and Bringing a Space to Life

2D · 2026

Having grown up moving between countries, including Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and other parts of the world, I have always experienced culture as something fluid, layered, and constantly shifting. Southeast Asian music, in particular, has stayed with me as a powerful sensory memory. Its rhythms, repetition, and tonal complexity feel both structured and deeply expressive. In this project, I wanted to explore how I could translate those sounds into something physical, something that can be seen, felt, and moved through.


I approached this work by breaking music down into its core elements: rhythm, repetition, variation, and pause. I began with simple geometric studies, using circles as a primary form. For me, the circle represents continuity and cyclical motion, mirroring the repetitive structures I hear in Southeast Asian music. Through grids and iterations, I experimented with how these forms could expand, overlap, and create visual "rhythms." These early studies became a way of mapping sound into space, almost like creating my own visual language of music.


As my process developed, I found myself moving away from rigid geometry toward more organic and fluid forms. This shift felt natural because it reflects the way music itself moves between structure and freedom. The shapes began to stretch, connect, and soften, suggesting movement, resonance, and flow. I was also influenced by Southeast Asian art and architecture, especially the way patterns repeat and spaces feel both intricate and immersive.


In the final stage, I translated these ideas into a spatial installation designed for both performance and physical experience. I wanted the space to feel immersive and almost sculptural, where people do not just observe but move through it. The large, curved forms create a sense of enclosure and intimacy, while smaller elements echo the fragmented rhythms I explored earlier. I imagine this space as something that holds sound, movement, and presence all at once.


Ultimately, this project is about translation and identity. As someone who has grown up between cultures, I am interested in how experiences, especially sensory ones like music, can be carried, transformed, and re-expressed in new ways. This work reflects my attempt to make something intangible tangible and to create a space where sound, memory, and form come together.
