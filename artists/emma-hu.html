Emma Hu

Constructed Topography

2D, 3D · 2026

It started with a question: how can architecture serve as a record of what has already occurred and vanished?


"Strata" became a way of approaching this, understanding it not just as layers but as a condition in which different moments and systems coexist without fully replacing one another. Rather than beginning with isolated forms, the work focuses on what already exists. In Constructed Topography, this appears as terrain, where architecture emerges from the land and follows its movement, allowing circulation to blend with topography. In Reimagine Elefsina: What Time Left Behind, it appears as time itself, with the site seen as a collection of histories visible through fragments rather than a single one. In Stratification, it appears as a process through cutting, weaving, and repetition—in which form develops gradually through layering rather than being imposed.


From landscape to history, architecture becomes less about creating something new and more about revealing and reorganizing what's already there. It preserves traces of terrain, time, and human activity. It holds multiple layers of what has been left behind, what continues to change, and what's still emerging.
